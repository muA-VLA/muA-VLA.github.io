µA-VLA:
A Multi-Agent Online Self-Healing Framework for Long-Horizon Robotic Manipulation

Ziheng Liu1, You Wang2, Jun Fu1
1Zhejiang Gongshang University   2Luoteng Technology (Hangzhou) Co., Ltd. & Zhejiang University
Paper Code Video

Abstract

We introduce µA-VLA, a multi-agent online self-healing framework for long-horizon robotic manipulation. µA-VLA couples failure reasoning grounded in failure visual observations with dynamic, code-driven task intervention in a reasoning–coding–intervention loop, enabling recovery from execution errors and continued task progress.

Method

µA-VLA forms a closed loop that turns failure analysis into executable online interventions. The framework maintains a structured record of failure causes and synthesizes runnable recovery code to guide the underlying VLA policy during execution.

Results

On CALVIN ABC→D, µA-VLA achieves state-of-the-art performance with an Avg. Len. of 4.76 and 90.1% 5-step success, and shows robust long-horizon deployment in real-world experiments.
